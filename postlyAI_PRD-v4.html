Section 01

Why v4 Exists

v3 fixed the biggest structural problems. These are the remaining ones. All small. All important.

Still wrong: calling it a "Content Calendar"

A content calendar sounds like: Monday post this, Tuesday post that. Business owners don't care about content. They care about selling. The product is now called a Weekly Marketing Plan throughout. That framing shifts the product from a posting tool to a marketing assistant — harder to replace with free AI.

Still wrong: the calendar generates generic ideas

"Tuesday: Customer testimonial." This is not valuable enough. After two weeks it feels repetitive. The AI must generate specific, actionable, business-context-aware ideas — not generic marketing themes. See Section 04 for how this is fixed with a monthly goal and business context engine.

Still wrong: requiring signup before showing value

Current flow: landing page → signup → profile → then value. That's too much friction for a business owner who hasn't yet trusted you. New flow: landing page → "What do you sell?" → see a sample week instantly → then signup. Value before commitment.

Still wrong: ₦5,000 may be too high for an unknown product

Not because ₦5,000 is expensive — it's 3% of what a good SMM charges. But unknown products don't get trusted easily. Test ₦2,500 beta and ₦3,500 early adopter pricing first. Increase once you have testimonials and case studies.

Still wrong: "Nigerian context" is a promise you must actually keep

Claiming "understands Nigerian culture, slang, seasons" sounds good until a user tests it and gets generic output. This must be hardcoded into the prompt: Eid, Sallah, Christmas, back-to-school, payday week, owambe season, Valentine campaigns. If you claim it, build it.

What this version fixes

"Content Calendar" → "Weekly Marketing Plan" throughout. Generic calendar ideas → business-goal-driven specific campaigns. Signup-first → value-first. Conservative launch pricing. Nigerian context built into prompts, not just claimed.

Section 04

MVP Feature Set — Final

Four features. Nothing else until you have 10+ paying customers telling you what to add next.

F-01 Business Profile + Goal Setup MVP Core 1–2 days

"I describe my business once and set my goal for this month. The app remembers everything."

  • Email/password signup via Supabase Auth
  • Business profile: name, industry, what they sell, target customer, tone
  • Monthly goal (new in v4): Get more enquiries / Increase sales / Grow followers / Launch product / Retain customers
  • Profile saved to Supabase; injected into every AI prompt as context
  • Editable from settings at any time — goal should update monthly
Why the monthly goal matters

Without a goal, the AI plans generic content. With "product launch" as a goal, it plans teaser posts → reveal → urgency → follow-up. The goal turns a content plan into a marketing campaign.

F-02 Weekly Marketing Plan — The Product MVP Core 3 days

"Every Monday I see exactly what to post each day this week — not just themes, actual specific ideas for my business."

  • Home screen — the first thing after login, every session
  • 7-day card list: each card shows Day / Platform / Specific idea / Post type (Promo, Story, Engagement, Campaign)
  • Ideas are specific to industry + current week + monthly goal + Nigerian calendar context
  • Nigerian seasons hardcoded in prompts: Eid/Sallah, Christmas, back-to-school, Valentine's, payday week (25th–5th), owambe season (Dec–Jan), Black Friday (Nov)
  • Auto-regenerates every Monday; also has "Regenerate week" button
  • Completed days show ✓ (marked manually by user)
  • Saved in Supabase calendars table with week_start key
Specific vs generic — the difference that drives retention

Generic (bad): "Tuesday: Customer testimonial." — Same every week. User stops opening. Specific (good): "Tuesday: Post the DM from your customer who wore your lace to a wedding and ask followers to share theirs." — User comes back because this is valuable enough to act on.

F-03 One-Tap Content Generation MVP Core 3–4 days

"I tap Monday's idea and get an Instagram caption, WhatsApp message, and 12 hashtags — all in one go."

  • Tap any day in the plan → generation panel opens, pre-filled with that day's specific idea
  • Single "Write it" button — no platform picker, no extra steps
  • One streamed AI response with three clearly labelled sections: INSTAGRAM CAPTION → WHATSAPP VERSION → HASHTAGS
  • Copy button per section + "Copy all" button
  • Optional "Add detail" field: price, offer deadline, location
  • Tone switcher: regenerate in different tone without re-entering input
  • Caption rewriter: paste existing caption → AI improves it
  • Generation auto-saved and linked to that day's plan item
Why one output, not a platform picker

v2 made users choose a platform first. That's friction. The business owner wants to post everywhere. Generate Instagram + WhatsApp + hashtags in one response and let them copy what they need. One less decision = more likely to use it daily.

F-04 Subscription & Billing MVP Core 3 days

"Paying is easy and my access reflects my plan. I'm not hit with a hard wall — just a clear nudge to upgrade."

  • Paystack recurring monthly subscription
  • Free: 7 generations total — enough to experience one full week before deciding to pay
  • Beta (₦2,500/month): 30 generations + full plan — for first 50 users only
  • Starter (₦5,000/month): 30 generations + full plan — standard pricing after beta
  • Growth (₦12,000/month): 80 generations + priority support + early Phase 2 access
  • Generation counter in Supabase, enforced server-side
  • Paystack webhook updates subscription status
  • Upgrade prompt is inline and contextual — never a hard block

Section 05

Onboarding — Value Before Friction

The biggest conversion lever is showing the product before asking for a signup. Do this.

5.1 The corrected landing page flow

1
Landing page — "What does your business sell?"

Single text field on the landing page. No signup yet. Just a text box and a button: "See your sample week." This is the hook. No commitment required.

2
Instant sample week — value before signup

AI generates a sample 7-day marketing plan based on what they typed. They can see the output. Real, specific content ideas for their type of business. This is the moment of trust.

3
Signup prompt — "Save this and get your real plan"

After seeing the sample: "Sign up to save this and generate real content from it." Now they understand the value and are motivated to create an account. Conversion is much higher here than at a cold landing page.

4
Full profile — fill in business details

After signup: business name, industry, target customer, tone, monthly goal. AI regenerates the plan with real context. This is the "aha moment" — the plan now feels tailored and specific.

5
First generation — within 90 seconds of signing up

Onboarding ends with the user tapping a day in their plan and generating their first piece of content. They must hit the core loop before they close the tab. If they get to this step, retention is dramatically higher.

Why this matters

Cold signup conversion rates on SaaS are typically 2–5%. Showing value before signup can push this to 15–30%. The business owner already trusts the product before they create an account. That trust carries over to payment.

5.2 Target: first generation under 90 seconds

From landing on the site to generating the first piece of real content: under 90 seconds. If it takes longer, simplify the profile form. If onboarding is complex, users leave. Track time-to-first-generation as a primary metric from day one.

6.2 The AI prompt — with Nigerian context hardcoded

This is where the "Nigerian context" claim becomes real, not just a tagline.

app/api/generate/route.ts — simplified, Nigerian-context-aware 
import { streamText } from 'ai'
import { openai } from '@ai-sdk/openai'

// Nigerian calendar context — hardcoded, not AI-guessed
const NIGERIAN_CONTEXT = {
  jan:  'New Year, Detty December hangover, fresh start campaigns',
  feb:  "Valentine's Day — big for fashion, food, beauty, gift sellers",
  mar:  'End of first quarter, spring energy',
  apr:  'Easter — family spending, food, clothing',
  may:  'Ramadan (varies), modest fashion, food businesses',
  jun:  'School is out, family time, mid-year',
  jul:  'Sallah / Eid celebrations — massive for fashion & food',
  aug:  'Back-to-school — clothing, stationery, services',
  sep:  'Back-to-school wind-down, Q4 buildup',
  oct:  'Pre-Black Friday, year-end planning',
  nov:  'Black Friday, pre-Christmas shopping surge',
  dec:  'Christmas, Detty December, owambe season — biggest month for most businesses',
  payday: 'Payday week (25th–5th): people are spending. Push promos.',
}

const GOAL_CAMPAIGN_MAP: Record<string, string> = {
  more_enquiries: 'Create curiosity and desire. End every post with a CTA to DM or WhatsApp.',
  increase_sales: 'Push urgency, scarcity, and value. Promos, bundles, limited offers.',
  grow_followers: 'Engaging, shareable content. Questions, polls, relatable moments.',
  launch_product: 'Week 1: teaser. Week 2: reveal. Week 3: launch & urgency. Week 4: social proof.',
  retain_customers: 'Appreciation posts, behind-the-scenes, loyalty offers, personal stories.',
}

export async function POST(req: Request) {
  const { calendarIdea, extraDetail, profile, mode } = await req.json()
  const month = new Date().toLocaleString('en', { month: 'short' }).toLowerCase()
  const seasonContext = NIGERIAN_CONTEXT[month as keyof typeof NIGERIAN_CONTEXT] || ''
  const goalStrategy = GOAL_CAMPAIGN_MAP[profile.goal] || ''

  const system = `You are a senior Nigerian marketing consultant.
Business: ${profile.name} | Industry: ${profile.industry}
Sells: ${profile.whatTheySell} | Target: ${profile.targetCustomer} | Tone: ${profile.tone}
Goal this month: ${profile.goal} — Strategy: ${goalStrategy}
Current Nigerian context: ${seasonContext}
Write like a local who understands Nigerian buying behaviour, culture, and slang where appropriate.
Be specific. Generic advice is useless.`

  // PLAN generation — produces specific, goal-driven weekly ideas
  if (mode === 'plan') {
    const planPrompt = `Generate a 7-day marketing plan (Monday to Sunday) for this Nigerian ${profile.industry} business.

Their goal this month: ${goalStrategy}
Current season/context: ${seasonContext}

Rules:
- Each idea must be SPECIFIC to this exact business and what they sell — not generic marketing advice
- Each idea should directly support their monthly goal
- Vary post types: product showcase, customer story, offer/promo, education, behind-the-scenes, engagement
- Include at least one WhatsApp broadcast day and one sales/offer day
- Make the ideas concrete enough that a content creator could execute without asking questions

Format each day EXACTLY like this:
DAY: Monday
PLATFORM: Instagram
TYPE: Product showcase
IDEA: [The specific, actionable idea — 1–2 sentences]
---`

    const result = streamText({
      model: openai('gpt-4o-mini'),
      system, prompt: planPrompt, maxTokens: 800,
    })
    return result.toDataStreamResponse()
  }

  // CONTENT generation — from a calendar idea
  const contentPrompt = `
The business needs to post about: "${calendarIdea}"
${extraDetail ? `Extra detail: ${extraDetail}` : ''}

Write all three in one response:

**INSTAGRAM CAPTION**
[Compelling caption with emojis. Brand tone. Clear CTA. Feels Nigerian — not corporate.]

**WHATSAPP VERSION**
[Same message, more conversational. Feels like a message from a trusted friend who runs a business.
Personal opener, body, CTA. No spam energy.]

**HASHTAGS**
[12 hashtags — Nigerian location tags, industry-specific, broad reach.
Example mix: #LagosStyle #AbujaFashion #NigerianBusiness + niche + broad]`

  const result = streamText({
    model: openai('gpt-4o-mini'),
    system, prompt: contentPrompt, maxTokens: 600,
  })
  return result.toDataStreamResponse()
}

6.3 Database schema — 4 tables, nothing more

-- auth.users managed by Supabase

-- Business profiles (1 per user)
create table profiles (
  id         uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id    uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade unique,
  name       text not null,
  industry   text not null,
  what_sells text not null,
  target     text not null,
  tone       text not null default 'Professional',
  goal       text not null default 'increase_sales',
  -- goal options: more_enquiries | increase_sales | grow_followers 
  --               | launch_product | retain_customers
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Weekly marketing plans
create table plans (
  id         uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id    uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  week_start date not null,
  -- items: [{id, day, platform, type, idea, completed, generation_id}]
  items      jsonb not null default '[]',
  created_at timestamptz default now(),
  unique(user_id, week_start)
);

-- All AI generations
create table generations (
  id           uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id      uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  plan_item_id text,        -- linked to plan item
  idea_prompt  text not null,
  extra_detail text,
  output       text not null,
  created_at   timestamptz default now()
);

-- Subscriptions
create table subscriptions (
  id              uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id         uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade unique,
  plan            text not null default 'free',
  paystack_sub_id text,
  status          text not null default 'active',
  gen_count       int default 0,
  period_start    timestamptz default now(),
  period_end      timestamptz
);

Why start at ₦2,500

Unknown products don't get trusted easily. Lower beta pricing removes risk for early adopters who haven't seen testimonials yet. Once you have 20+ paying users and 5+ video testimonials, raise to ₦5,000. Getting the first 50 customers matters more than maximizing early revenue.

7.4 Validation — do this before writing code

8.2 First 20 paying customers — the plan

Before you write a line of code

  • Post on Twitter/X: "Building an AI tool that plans your marketing week and writes it for you. Nigerian business owners — would you pay ₦2,500/month? Reply yes or no." Screenshot the replies.
  • DM 20 business owners personally. Ask: "Would you pay ₦2,500 for early access next month?" Track every yes with a name and number.
  • If you get 10+ yeses, build. If fewer than 5, the pitch needs work — not the product.

Build a waitlist landing page (day 1–2 of building)

  • Landing page with: headline, a screenshot or GIF of a sample weekly plan, 3 bullet benefits, email capture
  • Show the sample plan for a specific industry (fashion, food) — specific is more convincing than generic
  • Target: 50 waitlist signups before full launch

Soft launch (weeks 1–2 after build)

  • Give 5 beta users free access for 4 weeks in exchange for a 60-second video testimonial
  • Post a screen recording (no voiceover needed): landing page → type your business → see your week → tap a day → content generated. 30 seconds. Product sells itself.
  • Submit to TechCabal, Zikoko, BusinessDay for a mention

Month 2+ — building distribution

  • Partner with 2–3 Nigerian business coaches. Offer 30% recurring commission per referral. This is your most powerful growth lever.
  • Post 3×/week on Twitter: "This week's marketing plan for a Lagos fashion seller" — show a real generated plan, blur the business name. Show the product in the content.
  • ₦10,000 test Instagram ad to Lagos business owners aged 25–40 with a specific creative: "Stop staring at a blank phone. See what to post this week →"
  • Referral programme: one free month per paying referral
The most important rule

Your first 10 customers come from relationships, not ads. Every Nigerian entrepreneur knows 20 other Nigerian entrepreneurs. Start there. Distribution is almost always harder than building the product.

Section 10

Success Metrics

One primary metric. Seven supporting ones. Check weekly.

The one metric that matters above all others

% of active users who generate content from the plan at least twice per week. This number tells you if you've built a habit. If it's above 40%, you have something real. If it's below 20%, the plan ideas are not specific enough — that's the problem to fix.

Metric Target What it tells you
Weekly plan usage rate 40%+ generate 2×/week from plan The habit is forming. This is the leading indicator for everything.
MRR ₦200k by month 4 The business is alive
Free → paid conversion 15–25% The value is clear enough to pay for
Monthly churn Under 6% Users are getting ongoing value
Time to first generation Under 90 seconds from landing page Onboarding is working. If slow, simplify.
Monday plan open rate 60%+ of active users The weekly habit is real
NPS 40+ Worth recommending

Reading the signals

✅ The business is working
  • Users open the plan every Monday without being reminded
  • You get referrals you didn't ask for
  • Users complain when there's downtime
  • People ask for specific features — they care
  • Free users upgrade before you even reach out
  • Users update their monthly goal each month
🚨 Fix these immediately
  • Users sign up but never generate content
  • Users generate once and disappear
  • Monday plan open rate below 30%
  • Users say plan ideas feel generic or repetitive
  • Free trial ends with no upgrade after 2 weeks
  • Churn above 10% per month
If the plan ideas feel generic — this is the fix

Don't add more features. Make the AI smarter. Improve the plan generation prompt to use more profile detail, more seasonal context, and previous-week output. The quality of the plan is the product's core differentiator — it cannot be generic, or users will drift to ChatGPT.
