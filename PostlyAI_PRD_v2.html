Section 01

Executive Summary

What is Postly AI?

Postly AI is a web-based SaaS tool that uses AI to write marketing content for Nigerian small businesses. Instead of paying a social media manager ₦50,000–₦150,000/month, a business owner types one sentence and gets ready-to-post captions for Instagram, WhatsApp, Twitter/X, and SMS — plus hashtags, a weekly content calendar, and more — in seconds.

The one-line pitch

"Postly AI writes your marketing content so you can focus on running your business."

Why now?

  • Nigeria has over 40 million small businesses — most cannot afford a dedicated marketer.
  • Social media is now the primary sales channel for Nigerian SMEs (Instagram, WhatsApp, TikTok).
  • AI writing tools exist but are not tailored for Nigerian context, tone, or platforms.
  • The Vercel AI SDK makes streaming AI responses into a web app faster and cheaper than ever.
  • No competitor owns this space in Nigeria today — it is wide open.

Product Overview

3.1 What Postly AI does

Postly AI is a web app. A business owner signs up, tells the tool about their business once, then generates marketing content on demand. The AI knows their industry, tone, target customers, and the platform they are posting on — so every piece of content feels like it was written by a professional marketer who knows their brand.

Version 2 expands on the core generator with a content calendar, auto hashtags, a WhatsApp broadcast builder, a bio/profile writer, and a product description generator — all without adding complexity to the main user flow.

Section 04

Features & User Stories

4.1 Core MVP features — build these first

These are the only features needed to launch and charge money. Keep it lean.


F-01 User Authentication 2 days

"As a business owner, I want to create an account and log in securely so my content and settings are saved."

  • Email + password signup via Supabase Auth
  • Google OAuth login (optional in v1)
  • Protected dashboard routes using Next.js middleware
F-02 Business Profile Setup 1–2 days

"As a user, I want to tell the AI about my business once so every piece of content it generates already understands my brand."

  • Form: business name, industry, what they sell, target customer, tone (Fun / Professional / Casual / Bold)
  • Saved to Supabase on first login
  • Editable from settings at any time
  • Profile injected into every AI prompt automatically
F-03 AI Content Generator + Auto Hashtags Updated 3–4 days

"As a user, I want to type one sentence about my post and get 3 ready-to-use variations in seconds, with hashtags already included."

  • Content type selector: Instagram / WhatsApp / Twitter / SMS / Product spotlight
  • Single text input: "What is this post about?"
  • Optional: "Any extra detail?" (price, deadline, location)
  • Calls Vercel AI SDK streamText with full business profile context
  • Streams 3 variations live — user sees them generate in real time
  • Hashtag section auto-appears below each Instagram/Twitter variation — 10–15 tags (Nigerian niche + broad). One click to copy separately or together with caption.
  • Tone switcher button: regenerate same post in different tone (no extra input needed)
  • Caption rewriter: paste existing caption → AI improves it
  • Copy button per variation
UX note — hashtags

Hashtags appear in a collapsible section below each caption variant. Default: collapsed to keep the UI clean. User taps "Show hashtags (15)" to expand. This way the core flow stays simple but the value is always one tap away.

F-04 Content History 1–2 days

"As a user, I want to see all the content I have generated before so I can reuse or edit it."

  • All generations saved to Supabase with timestamp, content type, input, output
  • Simple list view — newest first
  • Copy and regenerate buttons on each item
  • Basic filter by content type
F-05 Weekly Content Calendar New 2–3 days

"As a user, I want a weekly plan showing what to post each day so I never run out of ideas and I come back to the app daily."

  • 7-day grid view (Mon–Sun) on the dashboard — the first thing users see after login
  • One "Plan my week" button: AI fills in 7 post ideas tailored to their industry, current week, and Nigerian calendar (Eid, Valentine's, Black Friday, back-to-school, etc.)
  • Each day shows: platform icon + one-line idea (e.g. "Instagram — Flash sale: 3 hours only")
  • Click any day → opens the generator pre-filled with that idea — user just hits Generate
  • Completed days show a ✓ checkmark (user marks manually — no scheduling needed)
  • Calendar regenerates each Monday automatically
  • Saved to Supabase — persists across sessions
Why this drives retention

Users who see their week planned out come back daily to generate that day's post. The calendar is the reason to open the app every morning — not just when they remember. This is the single highest-retention feature in v2.

F-06 WhatsApp Broadcast Builder New 2 days

"As a business owner, I want to write a WhatsApp broadcast that doesn't sound spammy and actually gets replies."

  • Separate tab or section in the generator: "Build a WhatsApp Broadcast"
  • Step 1 — Pick audience: Existing customers / New leads / Everyone
  • Step 2 — Pick goal: Announce promo / Share new product / General update / Re-engage cold contacts
  • Step 3 — One line of detail: "30% off all dresses this weekend only"
  • AI writes a broadcast message that feels personal and conversational, not copy-paste salesy
  • Output includes: greeting opener + body + clear CTA + emoji usage appropriate to Nigerian WhatsApp culture
  • Same streaming UI as the main generator
Why this is high value

Nigerian SMEs live on WhatsApp. A badly written broadcast gets ignored or gets the sender blocked. A well-written one drives direct sales. This feature alone is worth the subscription price for food and fashion sellers.

F-07 Bio & Profile Writer New 1 day

"As a user, I want my Instagram and WhatsApp Business bio to sound professional and describe exactly what I sell."

  • Accessible from the profile/settings area or as a one-time prompt on onboarding
  • Pick platform: Instagram Bio (150 chars) / WhatsApp Business description / Twitter/X bio
  • AI pre-fills using their saved business profile — user can add extra notes
  • Generates 3 bio options in different tones
  • Character counter for platform limits
  • One-time use mostly, but users return to refresh it periodically
F-08 Product Description Writer New 1 day

"As a fashion or food seller, I want a professional product description I can paste into Instagram, my website, or Jumia."

  • Input: product name, price, 2–3 key details (colour, size, ingredients, etc.)
  • Output: short punchy description (for Instagram) + longer detail version (for Jumia/website)
  • Automatically includes price formatting, CTA, and relevant emojis
  • Uses the saved business profile for brand tone consistency
  • No new database table needed — saved in the same generations table with type = "product_description"
F-09 Subscription & Billing 3–4 days

"As a user, I want to pay for my plan easily and have my account upgrade or restrict based on my subscription."

  • Paystack subscription integration (recurring monthly billing)
  • Four plans: Free, Starter, Growth, Pro
  • Free trial: 5 generations before paywall
  • Generation counter tracked in Supabase
  • Plan limits enforced server-side
  • Paystack webhook updates subscription status in DB
  • New features (Calendar, Broadcast Builder, Bio Writer, Product Desc) gated to Starter+ plan

5.2 Vercel AI SDK integration

The Vercel AI SDK handles streaming AI responses to the browser, making generation feel fast and live. The same route handles all content types — the prompt changes based on the type parameter.

Terminal — install dependencies 
npm install ai @ai-sdk/openai
app/api/generate/route.ts 
import { streamText } from 'ai'
import { openai } from '@ai-sdk/openai'

export async function POST(req: Request) {
  const { contentType, userInput, extraDetail, profile } = await req.json()

  const systemPrompt = `You are a professional Nigerian marketing copywriter.
Business: ${profile.businessName}
Industry: ${profile.industry}
They sell: ${profile.whatTheySell}
Target customer: ${profile.targetCustomer}
Tone: ${profile.tone}
Always write in a way that feels local, relatable, and drives action.`

  // Different prompt structure per content type
  const promptMap: Record<string, string> = {
    instagram: `Write 3 Instagram captions for: "${userInput}". 
                After each caption, add 12 relevant hashtags (mix of Nigerian, niche, broad).
                Format: Caption text\n\nHashtags: #tag1 #tag2 ...`,
    whatsapp:  `Write 1 WhatsApp broadcast message for: "${userInput}".
                Audience: ${extraDetail?.audience}. Goal: ${extraDetail?.goal}.
                Conversational, personal, not spammy. Include a clear CTA.`,
    bio:       `Write 3 bio options for their ${extraDetail?.platform} profile.
                Keep to character limit. Professional but approachable.`,
    product:   `Write a short Instagram description AND a longer Jumia/website description
                for this product: "${userInput}". Price: ${extraDetail?.price}.`,
    calendar:  `Plan 7 social media post ideas for a ${profile.industry} business.
                One idea per day (Monday to Sunday). Current week context: ${extraDetail?.weekContext}.
                Format: Day | Platform | One-line idea`,
    default:   `Write 3 variations of a ${contentType} post for: "${userInput}". 
                Number each (1. 2. 3.). Include a call to action in each.`
  }

  const result = streamText({
    model: openai('gpt-4o-mini'),
    system: systemPrompt,
    prompt: promptMap[contentType] ?? promptMap.default,
    maxTokens: 800,
  })

  return result.toDataStreamResponse()
}

5.3 Database schema

Four tables. No new tables needed for v2 features — content type field handles everything.

-- Table 1: Supabase Auth handles auth.users automatically

-- Table 2: business profiles (unchanged from v1)
create table profiles (
  id         uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id    uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  name       text not null,
  industry   text not null,
  what_sells text not null,
  target     text not null,
  tone       text not null,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Table 3: all generations (content_type covers all new features)
-- content_type values: instagram | whatsapp | twitter | sms | 
--   product_description | bio | whatsapp_broadcast | calendar
create table generations (
  id           uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id      uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  content_type text not null,
  user_input   text not null,
  extra_detail jsonb,           -- stores audience, goal, price, platform etc.
  output       text not null,
  created_at   timestamptz default now()
);

-- Table 4: subscriptions
create table subscriptions (
  id              uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id         uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  plan            text not null default 'free',
  paystack_sub_id text,
  status          text not null default 'active',
  gen_count       int default 0,
  period_start    timestamptz default now(),
  period_end      timestamptz
);

-- Table 5: weekly calendar (one row per user per week)
create table calendars (
  id         uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id    uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  week_start date not null,
  days       jsonb not null,    -- [{day, platform, idea, completed, generation_id}]
  created_at timestamptz default now(),
  unique(user_id, week_start)
);

5.4 API route structure

EndpointPurpose
POST /api/generateMain AI endpoint. Handles all content types via contentType param. Streams response.
GET/POST /api/profileFetch or create/update business profile.
GET /api/historyFetch user's generation history with optional type filter.
GET/POST /api/calendarGet this week's calendar or generate a new one.
PATCH /api/calendar/:idMark a calendar day as completed.
POST /api/webhooks/paystackReceives Paystack events to update subscription status in DB.
GET /api/planReturn current user plan and generation count for UI gating.

7.2 How to get your first 20 paying customers

Week 1–2 — Before launch

  • Post the problem on Twitter/X: "Why do Nigerian small businesses struggle to market consistently?"
  • Share build progress: "Building an AI tool that writes captions for Nigerian businesses"
  • DM 10 business owners you know personally. Ask: "Would you pay ₦5,000/month for AI that writes your captions?"
  • Post in Nigerian entrepreneur WhatsApp groups and Facebook groups

Week 3–4 — Soft launch

  • Give free 30-day access to 5 beta users in exchange for feedback and testimonials
  • Post a demo video on Twitter and Instagram showing the tool generating content live
  • Submit to Nigerian tech communities — Indie Hackers Nigeria, Tech Cabal
  • Write one blog post: "How Nigerian SMEs can post on Instagram every day without hiring a manager"

Month 2+ — Growth

  • Ask every happy user to refer one friend — offer 1 free month for each referral
  • Partner with business coaches and SME consultants who have audiences
  • Run targeted Instagram ads to Nigerian business owner accounts (₦10,000 test budget)
  • Post 3x/week on Twitter — marketing tips for Nigerian businesses, showing Postly AI solving them
The most important rule

Do not wait until the product is perfect to start selling. Start talking to potential customers before you write a single line of code. Your first 5 customers should come from your personal network. Build the product alongside real users — not in isolation.
