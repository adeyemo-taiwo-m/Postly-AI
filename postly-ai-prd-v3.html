Section 01

What v2 Got Wrong

This section exists because ignoring these problems means building the wrong product. Read it carefully.

Problem #1 — Too many features before proving demand

v2 listed 8 major features as "MVP." That's not an MVP. That's a full product. Most founders fail because they build 8 things before discovering nobody wants the first one. The corrected MVP has 3 things.

Problem #2 — "AI caption generator" is a feature, not a business

People don't buy captions. They buy more sales, more enquiries, more customers. The pitch "AI writes captions" has a free competitor at every corner: ChatGPT, Gemini, Meta AI, Claude. The pitch must shift to something harder to replicate.

Problem #3 — Revenue projections were fantasy

v2 projected 20 paying users in month 3 and 150 by month 12. Without an existing audience or distribution, a more realistic path is 5–15 paying users in month 3 and 50–100 by month 12. The corrected model reflects this honestly.

Problem #4 — Retention was not solved

If a user generates 20 captions in week one and saves them, why come back? Recurring SaaS needs a recurring reason to return. Recurring pain. The calendar is the answer — but v2 buried it under 7 other features instead of making it the product.

Problem #5 — Unlimited generations are not valuable

Most SMEs post 3–5 times per week — roughly 20 posts per month. Unlimited is not a selling point. It inflates your AI costs while selling something nobody actually needs. The Growth plan offering "unlimited" is not a meaningful upgrade from 30/month.

Section 02

The Real Competitive Problem

v2 treated Buffer and Hootsuite as competitors. They are not the threat. The real threat is this:

The question every potential customer will ask

Why should I pay ₦5,000/month when I can open ChatGPT and type "write me an Instagram caption for my new lace material"? For free. Every day.

This question must be answered — not for investors, not for developers — for the customer. If you can't answer it convincingly, the product has no durable reason to exist.

The real competitors

CompetitorWhat they offerWhy Postly must be different
ChatGPT (free)Writes any caption, for any business, instantlyNo Nigerian context, no business memory, no weekly plan, no structure — user must figure it all out themselves
Meta AI (WhatsApp)Built into WhatsApp. Free. Growing fast.Same: no business profile, no calendar, no marketing decisions
Claude / GeminiFree, capable AI writingSame: no structure, no Nigerian seasonal context, no retention mechanism
Hiring an SMM₦50k–₦150k/month for a humanPostly is 10–30× cheaper. This is the real comparison to make.
The actual answer — what free AI cannot do

Free AI will write captions when you remember to ask. Postly tells you what to post today, plans your week, understands your Nigerian business context, and becomes the habit of coming back every morning. The value is structure and guidance — not just text generation. That's what you are selling.

3.1 The product is now calendar-first

The weekly content calendar is no longer one feature among eight. It is the product. Everything else — caption generation, hashtags, WhatsApp broadcasts — flows out of the calendar. The user's first experience after signup is seeing their week planned for them.

3.3 The one workflow that matters

The core loop — this is all that needs to work perfectly

User opens app → sees their week → taps today's idea → AI writes the caption + WhatsApp version + hashtags in one output → user copies and posts → taps ✓ done → tomorrow they come back for the next one. That loop is the product. Everything else is secondary.

Section 04

Leaner Feature Set

Three things for MVP. Four if you're ahead of schedule. Nothing else until you have 10+ paying customers.

F-01 Business Profile Setup MVP Core 1–2 days

"As a user, I want to describe my business once and have the AI remember it forever."

  • Email/password signup via Supabase Auth
  • Onboarding form: business name, industry, what they sell, target customer, tone (Fun / Bold / Professional / Casual)
  • Profile saved to Supabase, injected into every AI prompt
  • Editable in settings — but users rarely need to touch it again
Onboarding insight

After signup, skip the dashboard entirely. Take the user straight to the profile form, then immediately generate their first week's calendar. First experience must show value within 90 seconds.

F-02 Weekly Content Calendar — The Product MVP Core 3 days

"As a user, I want to see exactly what to post each day this week so I never have to think about it."

  • This is the home screen — the first thing users see every session
  • 7-day grid (Mon–Sun) with one post idea per day, tailored to their industry
  • AI considers Nigerian seasonal context: Eid, Christmas, back-to-school, Black Friday, TGIF, payday week, etc.
  • Each day shows: platform icon + idea + estimated tone (Promo / Story / Engagement / Education)
  • Calendar auto-regenerates every Monday. Saved to Supabase with week_start key.
  • Completed days show a ✓ checkmark (user marks manually)
  • "Regenerate week" button if they don't like the suggestions
F-03 One-Click Content Generation from Calendar MVP Core 3–4 days

"I tap Tuesday's idea and the AI writes the caption, WhatsApp version, and hashtags — all at once."

  • Tap any calendar day → opens a generation panel pre-filled with that day's idea
  • Single "Write it" button — no extra steps required
  • AI generates three things in one streamed output: (1) Instagram caption, (2) WhatsApp version, (3) 12 hashtags
  • This replaces the "choose a platform" selector from v2 — the calendar already knows the platform
  • Optional: "Add a detail" field for price, deadline, offer specifics
  • Copy buttons per section. One-tap copy of everything together.
  • Tone switcher: regenerate same idea in different tone
  • Caption rewriter: paste existing caption → AI improves it
  • All generations saved automatically with timestamp and linked calendar day
Why one output for all three

v2 asked users to choose a platform first. That's friction. The user wants to post the same content everywhere. Generate Instagram + WhatsApp + hashtags together and let them choose what to copy. Fewer taps = more usage = better retention.

F-04 Subscription & Billing MVP Core 3 days

"I want to pay easily and have my access reflect my plan."

  • Paystack recurring monthly subscription
  • Free: 7 generations total (enough to experience one week) — no card required
  • Starter (₦5,000/month): 30 generations/month + full calendar
  • Growth (₦12,000/month): 80 generations/month + priority support
  • Generation counter tracked in Supabase, enforced server-side
  • Paystack webhook updates subscription status
  • Upgrade prompt appears inline — never a hard block, always a soft nudge

5.2 Simplified API route — one output, all platforms

v3 simplifies the API. Instead of a contentType selector, the calendar provides context and the route always generates the full package: Instagram caption + WhatsApp version + hashtags.

app/api/generate/route.ts 
import { streamText } from 'ai'
import { openai } from '@ai-sdk/openai'

export async function POST(req: Request) {
  const { calendarIdea, extraDetail, profile } = await req.json()

  const system = `You are a professional Nigerian marketing copywriter.
Business: ${profile.name}
Industry: ${profile.industry}
They sell: ${profile.whatTheySell}
Target customer: ${profile.targetCustomer}
Tone: ${profile.tone}
Write in a way that feels local, relatable, and drives action.
You understand Nigerian culture, slang, seasons, and buying behaviour.`

  const prompt = `
The business needs to post about: "${calendarIdea}"
${extraDetail ? `Extra detail: ${extraDetail}` : ''}

Write all three in one response:

**INSTAGRAM CAPTION**
[Write a compelling caption. Add relevant emojis. End with a clear CTA.]

**WHATSAPP VERSION**
[Write the same message in a more conversational, personal tone for WhatsApp broadcast. 
No corporate language. Feels like a message from a friend who happens to run a business.]

**HASHTAGS**
[12 hashtags: mix of Nigerian location tags, niche industry tags, and broad reach tags.
Format: #tag1 #tag2 #tag3 ...]
`

  const result = streamText({
    model: openai('gpt-4o-mini'),
    system,
    prompt,
    maxTokens: 600,
  })

  return result.toDataStreamResponse()
}

5.3 Minimal database schema

Three tables only. Simpler than v2. Calendar table is the most important.

-- auth.users managed by Supabase automatically

-- Business profile (1 per user)
create table profiles (
  id         uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id    uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade unique,
  name       text not null,
  industry   text not null,
  what_sells text not null,
  target     text not null,
  tone       text not null default 'Professional',
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- All AI generations (linked to calendar days)
create table generations (
  id              uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id         uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  calendar_day_id text,   -- references calendar.days[n].id
  idea_prompt     text not null,
  extra_detail    text,
  output          text not null,
  created_at      timestamptz default now()
);

-- Weekly calendar (one row per user per week)
create table calendars (
  id         uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id    uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade,
  week_start date not null,
  -- days: [{id, day, platform, idea, type, completed, generation_id}]
  days       jsonb not null default '[]',
  created_at timestamptz default now(),
  unique(user_id, week_start)
);

-- Subscription state
create table subscriptions (
  id              uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id         uuid references auth.users(id) on delete cascade unique,
  plan            text not null default 'free',   -- free | starter | growth
  paystack_sub_id text,
  status          text not null default 'active',
  gen_count       int default 0,
  period_start    timestamptz default now(),
  period_end      timestamptz
);

Why remove "unlimited"

30 generations/month = posting every day, twice. No Nigerian SME needs more than this in their first year. "Unlimited" is a cost centre disguised as a feature. Remove it. If heavy users hit the cap, charge them for add-on packs at ₦1,000 per 10 extra generations.

6.4 The validation test — do this before building

Can you get 10 business owners to prepay ₦2,500 today?

Not sign up. Not say "interesting." Pay — for a product that does not exist yet. If yes, build it. If nobody pays before the product exists, do not spend 6 weeks coding. Fix the pitch first. Distribution is almost always harder than building.

7.2 Getting first 10 paying customers

Before you write a line of code

  • Post on Twitter/X: "Thinking of building an AI tool that plans your weekly content and writes it for you. Would you pay ₦5,000/month for this? Nigerian business owners — reply yes or no."
  • DM 20 business owners you know personally. Ask: "Would you pay ₦2,500 now for early access if it was ready next month?"
  • Track every "yes" with their name and number. These are your first customers before you build.

Soft launch (weeks 1–2 after build)

  • Give 5 beta users free access for 4 weeks. Ask for a 60-second video testimonial in return.
  • Post a live screen recording showing the calendar → tap → content generated in 10 seconds. No voiceover needed — the product sells itself.
  • Submit to TechCabal, Zikoko, BusinessDay Digital for a mention. These drive Nigerian SME attention.

Month 2+ — building distribution

  • Partner with 2–3 Nigerian business coaches who have audiences. Offer 30% recurring commission on every customer they refer.
  • Post three times/week on Twitter: "Today's marketing tip for Nigerian business owners" — always showing Postly AI solving the problem at the end.
  • Run one targeted Instagram ad (₦10,000 test budget) to Lagos business owner accounts aged 25–40.
  • Referral incentive: one free month for every paying customer a user refers.
The most important rule

Do not wait for the product to be perfect. Your first 5 customers come from personal relationships, not ads. Start conversations today, before you build. The product should be built alongside real users — not in isolation.

The single most important metric

Calendar open rate on Monday morning. If 60%+ of your active users open the app to see their week every Monday, you have built a habit. Habits are very hard to cancel. Everything else — revenue, conversion, NPS — follows from this one number.
